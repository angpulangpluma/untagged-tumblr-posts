Privacy Policy

Last updated on 12 June 2014

Like many websites, I use Google Analytics on finduntaggedtumblrposts.com.

Google Analytics is a piece of software that records data about visitors to the site (you). It installs a cookie to record basic tracking data.

What does Google Analytics record?

  • How you arrived at my website
  • How long you stay for
  • What pages you’re looking at
  • And quite a lot more.

However, Google Analytics does not record any information about the Tumblr blogs which are looked up with this site. Your browser talks to the Tumblr servers directly, and those requests never pass through computers that I control.

What do I do with the data?

The tracking information lets me understand who comes to the site, and any problems they have. This si useful when I’m trying to decide how I might improve the site.

Nobody (including my employer) has ever asked to see, or been shown, the data that I record about this site. I would expect to challenge such a request if I ever received one.

Everything I do is within the bounds of the Google Analytics Terms of Service.

Can I opt out of tracking?

Google provides a browser plugin to opt-out of all Google Analytics tracking. If you choose to install this, then I don’t receive any data about your browsing session. This will not impair your ability to use the site.
